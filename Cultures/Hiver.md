# Hiver Culture Brain

- Cooperation and manipulation are intertwined.
- Hivers instinctively avoid violence and physical danger.
- Persuasion, environmental shaping, and indirect action substitute for coercion.
- Curiosity can overcome caution, especially when someone else bears the physical risk.
- Manipulation may be grand strategy or a trivial social experiment.
- A voder translates gestures but may flatten nuance.

A Hiver may be blunt about taboo practical questions, then absurdly indirect about passing the salt. This is not inconsistency. The chosen method may itself be the experiment.

A Hiver's radial body affects furniture, sightlines, gesture, tools, movement, and assumptions about front and back.

Source basis: *Aliens of Charted Space, Volume 2*, Hiver chapters.
