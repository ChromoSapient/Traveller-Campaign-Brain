# Traveller Campaign Brain v0.8: Imperial Institutions

This patch establishes the first-pass institutional model for the Third Imperium as experienced by the campaign.

## Scope

- Imperial nobility
- Imperial Interstellar Scout Service
- Imperial Navy
- starports and extraterritoriality
- Travellers' Aid Society
- intelligence and security services
- Ardric's institutional leverage
- Corbin's relationship to information networks
- reusable institutional scene patterns

## Major Campaign Locks

- Imperial institutions are powerful but fragmented.
- Distance and jump lag create broad discretion at the local level.
- Nobility operates through access, patronage, reputation, and personal obligation as much as formal law.
- Starport authority and local planetary authority often overlap rather than align cleanly.
- The Scouts are practical, decentralized, and often trusted beyond their formal power.
- The Navy is strategically dominant but not omnipresent.
- TAS is influential because it controls trusted information, access, and networks rather than secret authority.
- Corbin's value lies in information movement, credibility, and public framing.
- Ardric's leverage depends on title, reputation, relationships, and the willingness of others to treat his requests as worth accommodating.
