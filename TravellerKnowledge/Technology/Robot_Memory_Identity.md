# Robot Memory and Identity

## Four Layers

**Body:** chassis, sensors, manipulators, armor, locomotion, and power systems.

**Brain:** the physical and computational substrate of cognition.

**Memory:** experience, learned associations, relationships, preferences, and autobiographical continuity.

**Identity:** the continuing individual who interprets memory, makes choices, and claims a self.

These layers are related but not interchangeable.

## Repair and Consent

Ordinary repair restores function. Work that changes values, memories, preferences, emotional architecture, autonomy, or the ability to refuse commands requires consent whenever Roamer can provide it.

## Backups and Copies

A restored backup may be Roamer continuing after interruption, a successor with his memories, or a new person convinced he is the original. The campaign has not settled this question.

Two simultaneously active copies immediately begin becoming different people. Neither is automatically disposable.

## Memory Editing

Deletion or rewriting may function as medicine, coercion, punishment, evidence destruction, identity damage, or attempted murder. Intent and consent matter.

## Death

Do not define Roamer's death only by whether the chassis can restart. Relevant thresholds include irreversible brain destruction, loss of memory continuity, deletion of personality, or continued operation after identity destruction.
