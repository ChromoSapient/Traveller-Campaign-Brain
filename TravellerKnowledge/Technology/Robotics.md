# Robotics Knowledge Base

## Status

Initial convergence file.

## General Principle

Robots are widespread across industrialized Charted Space, but cultural acceptance varies sharply.

Technical capability and legal personhood are separate questions.

## Brain Complexity

Increasingly advanced robot brains move from rigid programming toward learning, improvisation, opinions, beliefs, self-modeling, and apparent self-awareness.

Scholarly disagreement over sentience often reflects political and economic interests as much as technical uncertainty.

## Social Roles

Robots commonly appear in:

- hazardous work
- logistics
- maintenance
- service labor
- security
- medicine
- transport
- military support

Their ubiquity does not imply equal legal or social treatment.

## Vulnerability

A robot may depend on:

- compatible parts
- sufficient local Tech Level
- maintenance intervals
- diagnostic access
- software integrity
- power systems
- legal registration

These create story consequences without requiring arbitrary malfunction.

## Personhood

A self-aware robot may demonstrate:

- preferences
- memory continuity
- moral judgment
- self-preservation
- identity claims
- relationship attachment

The Imperium may still classify that being as property.

## Roamer Application

Roamer's development should make him more individual, not more human.

His conflicts should often arise from:

- autonomy versus ownership
- memory access versus bodily integrity
- experimentation versus consent
- repair dependence versus personal freedom
- legal identity versus lived personhood
