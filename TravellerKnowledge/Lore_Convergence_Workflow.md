# Lore Convergence Workflow

## Goal

Produce one campaign-usable synthesis without pretending every edition agrees.

## Process

### 1. Define the Question

Examples:

- How do Vargr establish status?
- How are robots treated under Imperial law?
- What does a minor Imperial noble actually control?
- How does jump week affect crew behavior?

### 2. Gather Sources

Search:

- current-edition core and specialist books
- older edition setting books
- JTAS and other periodicals
- adventures
- campaign transcripts and rulings

### 3. Extract Claims

Each claim is paraphrased into a stable fact record with provenance.

### 4. Group Results

Sort claims into:

- consensus
- complementary detail
- edition-specific interpretation
- contradiction
- timeline-specific material
- mechanics-only difference

### 5. Adopt Campaign Interpretation

The referee may:

- adopt one version
- combine compatible versions
- preserve regional variation
- leave the matter unresolved

### 6. Update Dependencies

Record every character, culture, location, organization, and story rule affected by the adopted interpretation.

## Preferred Resolution Strategies

1. Regional variation
2. Historical change
3. Different social classes
4. Institutional versus popular belief
5. Observer bias
6. Explicit referee choice

A contradiction is often more useful than a deletion.
