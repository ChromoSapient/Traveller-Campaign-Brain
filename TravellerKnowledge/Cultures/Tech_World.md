# Tech World Culture

## Status

Campaign-oriented synthesis. Published-source extraction remains incomplete.

Tech World is a culture where advanced robotics, fabrication, testing, repair, and experimental systems are ordinary.

Likely cultural pressures include:

- expertise as status
- prototype prestige
- normalized diagnostics
- blurred lines between research and commerce
- comfort replacing hardware
- institutional ownership of designs and software
- belief that technical access is justified by competence

## Ethical Blind Spot

A culture accustomed to opening machines may fail to notice when inspection becomes violation.

Tech World professionals may sincerely believe that all systems should be auditable, hidden code is dangerous, maintenance access outranks privacy, ownership follows manufacture, and copying preserves identity.

These are cultural pressures, not universal truths.

## Roamer

Roamer's Tech World background supports his precision, directness, mission orientation, curiosity, respect for expertise, and early assumption that knowledge should be openly pursued.
