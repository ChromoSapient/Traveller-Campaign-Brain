# Aslan Cultural Framework

Aslan honor concerns obligation, courage, reputation, protection, and conduct recognized by others.

Clan and kin shape status, duty, patronage, marriage, and long-term responsibility.

Land symbolizes independence, continuity, and responsibility. For spacers, symbolic equivalents may include a ship, galley, post, or household.

Traditional Aslan gender roles are strong but should be treated as cultural expectations rather than identical personal beliefs.

Dralan expresses Aslan culture through life-debt, direct loyalty, courage, hospitality, and a willingness to place himself between danger and companions.
