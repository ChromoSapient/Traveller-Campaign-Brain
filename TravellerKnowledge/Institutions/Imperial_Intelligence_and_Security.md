# Imperial Intelligence and Security

## Fragmentation

The Imperium does not operate through one all-seeing intelligence service.

Relevant actors may include:

- naval intelligence
- subsector or sector authorities
- noble households
- corporate security
- planetary services
- special commissions
- military police
- Scout contacts
- unofficial patronage networks
- Omicron-like secretive divisions

## Information Problems

Jump lag creates:

- stale orders
- contradictory reports
- local initiative
- duplicated investigations
- jurisdictional rivalry
- deliberate compartmentalization
- opportunities for deception

## Campaign Use

Security organizations should feel dangerous because they possess:

- resources
- legal cover
- surveillance
- specialists
- detention authority
- access to classified information

They should not feel omniscient.

## Omicron Principle

A secretive organization can be powerful and still be constrained by:

- secrecy
- chain of command
- limited personnel
- political risk
- interdepartmental competition
- incomplete knowledge
- fear of exposure
