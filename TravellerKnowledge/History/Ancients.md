# Ancients Knowledge Base

## Epistemic Rule

Most claims about the Ancients are uncertain, politicized, fraudulent, incomplete, or misunderstood.

This uncertainty is a feature of the setting.

## Broad Consensus

Commonly accepted claims include:

- Ancient activity occurred hundreds of thousands of years ago
- many species were transplanted or altered
- large-scale destruction coincides with their disappearance
- surviving technology is often incomprehensible
- false finds and deliberate fraud are common

## Writing Rule

Never treat "Ancient" as a complete explanation.

For every supposed Ancient phenomenon, separate:

1. observation
2. identification claim
3. working interpretation
4. actual truth
5. who benefits from the interpretation

## Campaign Application

The campaign's repeated false artifacts and planted evidence should remain distinct from genuine Ancient material.

Fraud, misidentification, political manipulation, and incomplete science should all be viable explanations until evidence narrows them.
