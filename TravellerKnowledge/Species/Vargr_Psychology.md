# Vargr Psychology

## Core Distinction

Vargr psychology includes both inherited tendencies and learned social systems.

Inherited tendencies may include:

- sensitivity to confidence and uncertainty
- rapid pack formation
- strong response to reputation
- awareness of audience reaction
- desire for recognition
- leadership and affiliation drives
- expressive emotional signaling

Learned systems include:

- formal Charisma contests
- conventions of challenge and concession
- expectations of leaders and followers
- pack etiquette
- reputation transmission
- regional and subcultural norms

## Charisma

Charisma is not simply attractiveness or persuasion.

It is a socially recognized measure of:

- prestige
- authority
- confidence
- reputation
- demonstrated success
- ability to attract followers
- ability to keep followers

A Charisma exchange often has an audience, even when only two Vargr appear to be speaking.

## Pack Formation

A pack is a relationship structure, not merely a biological family.

Vargr may form packs around:

- kinship
- work
- military service
- crime
- survival
- travel
- politics
- shared danger
- friendship

Mixed-species packs are entirely plausible, especially in Imperial space.

## Leadership

Vargr leadership must remain credible.

Credibility can come from:

- success
- courage
- generosity
- judgment
- style
- protection
- decisiveness
- the confidence of followers

A leader need not be loud or domineering. A quiet leader whose decisions repeatedly work may accumulate enormous standing.

## Failure and Humiliation

Failure matters most when it is witnessed, retold, or interpreted as evidence of weakness.

Humiliation is social damage, not merely wounded pride.

## Loyalty

Vargr loyalty is often mischaracterized as unstable.

A better model:

- weakly grounded allegiance may shift quickly
- personal loyalty can be intense
- loyalty survives when the relationship remains meaningful and credible
- institutions are strongest when they continually reproduce personal trust and prestige

## Ruuk

Ruuk has strong social instincts but incomplete cultural literacy.

He can:

- feel pressure
- notice who is watching
- detect confidence or uncertainty
- recognize that a social move occurred

He may not know:

- what exact challenge was issued
- whether he was expected to concede
- how his answer changed standing
- why other Vargr now treat him differently

His practical competence may win Charisma contests he did not realize he had entered.
