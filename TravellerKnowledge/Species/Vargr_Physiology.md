# Vargr Physiology

## Status

Campaign-usable synthesis with source-confidence markers. Detailed Mongoose 1e page extraction remains queued.

## Baseline

Vargr are uplifted descendants of Terran canids, altered by the Ancients and established as a distinct sophont species.

## Senses

Vargr commonly possess heightened sensory capability compared with baseline humans, especially in scent and hearing.

Campaign use:

- scent can reveal stress, blood, machinery, smoke, unfamiliar individuals, and environmental contamination
- hearing can detect distant movement, machinery irregularities, breathing, and subtle vocal shifts
- neither sense is magical
- sealed suits, ventilation, masking agents, crowding, and environmental noise can defeat or confuse them

Ruuk's heightened senses are already reflected mechanically through his Recon bonus.

## Body Language

Vargr expression is multimodal.

Relevant channels include:

- ears
- tail
- posture
- weight distribution
- muzzle and lips
- fur response
- gaze
- vocal rhythm
- scent

A Vargr may consciously control some channels while leaking information through others.

## Locomotion and Balance

Vargr are generally agile, digitigrade, and well suited to rapid directional change.

Ruuk's Dustfall dance training is a cultural and personal specialization layered over this natural mobility. It is not a universal Vargr trait.

## Development

Biology does not transmit social literacy.

A Vargr child may naturally respond to confidence, threat, affiliation, and group attention while still requiring cultural teaching to interpret formal status behavior.

This distinction is central to Ruuk.

## Reproduction and Radiation

No campaign fact currently establishes Lurrza's diagnosis.

The campaign interpretation is:

- she has few known biological children
- she has spent years performing exterior and life-support work
- cumulative radiation exposure may have made later pregnancy dangerous
- this remains plausible and unconfirmed

Do not narrate infertility, miscarriage, or a specific medical condition without a future referee ruling.
