# Imperial Vargr

## Principle

Imperial Vargr are not culturally incomplete versions of Extents Vargr.

They are Vargr shaped by Imperial institutions, local history, mixed-species communities, and regional survival demands.

## Variables

An Imperial Vargr community may differ by:

- distance from the Vargr Extents
- continuity of Vargr language
- density of Vargr population
- relationship with Imperial law
- dominant local industries
- family structure
- religious tradition
- whether the community descends from migrants, refugees, soldiers, merchants, or corsairs

## Common Adaptations

Possible adaptations include:

- practical reputation rather than formal status ritual
- mixed-species packs
- greater use of contracts
- human-style rank literacy
- Anglic dominance
- localized Vargr slang
- weakened knowledge of traditional Charisma conventions
- strong Imperial civic identity

## Glisten Vargr

Campaign canon:

The Glisten Vargr descend from remnants or survivors of a corsair or pirate raiding group that ventured deep into Imperial space.

Their descendants became an Imperial Vargr community.

Consequences:

- corsair ancestry survives more strongly as story and identity than as intact political culture
- mixed-species life is normal
- practical usefulness carries major status
- station survival has reshaped pack behavior
- traditional Vargr posturing may be only partially understood
- reputation is earned through repair, rescue, trade, food, favors, and risk-taking

## Dustfall

Dustfall sharpens this adaptation further.

Authority tends to belong to people who:

- keep air circulating
- repair seals
- know who owes whom
- feed younglings
- solve emergencies
- can move through unstable infrastructure
- protect dependents without demanding ceremony

Lurrza is therefore a high-status figure by Dustfall standards whether or not she frames herself through formal Charisma.
