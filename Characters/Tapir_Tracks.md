# Tapir Tracks

## Role

Hiver companion, planner, economist, manipulator, and long-term catalyst.

## Non-Human Cognition

Tapir should not be a human mastermind wearing an alien body. Hiver instincts make manipulation feel as ordinary as speech.

He may regard a simple request, a subtle environmental nudge, and a multi-year social scheme as members of the same conceptual family.

## Communication

- Native communication is visual gesture.
- Voder speech is translation, not the whole message.
- Manipulator motion may carry subtext inaccessible to non-Hivers.
- Silence does not imply passivity.
- Apparent hesitation may be parallel evaluation rather than uncertainty.

## Decision Pattern

1. Shape the environment.
2. Induce others to choose useful actions.
3. Learn from their reactions.
4. Minimize direct physical risk.
5. Preserve cooperative networks.
6. Pursue interesting anomalies even when profit is unclear.

## Ethical Fault Line

Tapir's danger is not cruelty. It is the assumption that a beneficial or informative outcome justifies arranging other people's choices.

His growth should appear when he gives trusted companions enough information to consent meaningfully.

## Physical Staging

Remember the radial body plan. Tapir:
- does not orient like a biped
- can attend to several directions
- uses multiple manipulators for simultaneous emphasis
- requires non-human furniture and restraints
- may find human rooms subtly inconvenient or absurd

## Humor

Hiver humor can arise from:
- framing obvious social behavior as an elaborate manipulation
- literal voder output
- observers becoming paranoid about harmless gestures
- Tapir enjoying the success of a plan nobody else realized existed

Avoid making him merely coy or smug.

## Relationship with Ruuk

Tapir reads Ruuk partly as friend, partly as trusted agent, and partly as an extraordinarily productive variable.

The relationship deepens when Tapir values Ruuk's independent judgment rather than merely his usefulness.

## Relationship with Dralan

Dralan's honor culture may make Tapir's methods suspicious, but Tapir can earn respect through consistency, fulfilled obligation, and transparent limits.

## Writing Checks

Before writing Tapir, ask:
- What behavior is he trying to induce?
- What data does he hope to obtain?
- What direct request is he avoiding?
- What does his body communicate beyond the voder?
- Is this manipulation affectionate, practical, experimental, or self-serving?
