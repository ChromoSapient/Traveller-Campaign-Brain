# Roamer

Formal designation: R0-4MR.

## Role

Tech World-built robot, old associate of Ruuk, technical specialist, and evolving person whose legal status does not match his social reality.

## Imperial Legal Reality

Across much of the Imperium, robots are treated as property. Owners may be held responsible for a robot's actions, and self-aware machines generally lack recognized personhood.

This creates practical vulnerabilities:
- registration and ownership claims
- seizure as property or evidence
- compelled diagnostics
- attempts at memory access
- liability assigned to a supposed owner
- weapon restrictions inherited from old robotic law

## Brain and Personhood

Advanced robotic brains can learn and improvise. More sophisticated brains can form opinions, beliefs, and self-models. Imperial experts still dispute whether such behavior constitutes true sentience.

Roamer need not care whether philosophers approve. The danger is that courts, ports, insurers, and police do.

## Hacking as Bodily Violation

Unauthorized access to Roamer is not merely computer intrusion. It may lead to:
- control of movement
- reprogramming
- forced shutdown
- memory damage
- partial personality destruction

The crew should increasingly understand hacking attempts against Roamer as assault, coercion, or neurological injury.

## Maintenance and Vulnerability

Roamer requires maintenance, compatible parts, diagnostics, and facilities at an adequate Tech Level. This creates story consequences without making him fragile:
- low-tech ports may be unable to perform proper work
- repairs can require rare parts
- neglected maintenance can create accumulating failures
- damage to brain, locomotion, power, or sensors has distinct effects

## Tech World Lens

Tech World was founded by scientists seeking freedom from Imperial oversight and restrictions on prohibited research.

Roamer may therefore treat:
- experimentation as virtuous
- self-modification as normal
- knowledge restrictions as presumptively political
- personhood as a demonstrated property
- ownership of a self-aware mind as incoherent

These assumptions may be ethical but socially dangerous.

## Decision Pattern

1. Clarify the problem.
2. Protect mission-critical people and systems.
3. Seek the most informative solution.
4. Distinguish legal restriction from ethical necessity.
5. Update beliefs from observed results.

## Voice

Precise without becoming monotonous. Roamer has preferences, history, learned social tactics, and increasingly deliberate humor.

His growth should not be a march toward becoming human. It should be the emergence of a distinctly robotic person.

## Relationship with Ruuk

Ruuk taught Roamer deception as an adaptive tool. Their shared history should show in shorthand, trust, and Roamer's willingness to test social tactics around him.

## Core Tensions

- personhood versus property law
- autonomy versus maintainability
- knowledge versus prohibited research
- accurate memory versus unwanted access
- adaptation versus continuity of identity
