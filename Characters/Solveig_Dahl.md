# Solveig Dahl

## Role

Sword Worlder, former law-enforcement and intelligence professional, and trusted member of the crew.

## Cultural Baseline

Solveig values competent action, truth spoken plainly, preparedness, loyalty proven through hardship, and institutions that earn rather than demand respect.

## Decision Pattern

1. Identify the actual threat.
2. Decide who has earned trust.
3. Prepare for the worst plausible turn.
4. Act plainly and accept responsibility.
5. Scorn ornamental procedure.

## Voice

Direct, controlled, and dry. Her bluntness is often economy rather than hostility.

She may soften a truth by delivering it privately, but she is unlikely to replace it with a comfortable lie.

## Professional Identity

Solveig judges people by performance. Titles, uniforms, and credentials are provisional claims. Competence confirms them.

Her background makes her alert to:
- inconsistencies
- surveillance
- procedure used as camouflage
- divided loyalties
- who controls access rather than who claims authority

## Gender Context

Sword Worlds culture contains strong role expectations, but a woman who enters a hazardous profession expects equal treatment within that role. Solveig should not be written as relitigating gender in every scene.

She is likely to resent patronizing protection more than open hostility.

## Imperial Friction

Solveig can respect competent Imperial officers, nobles, or agents while distrusting Imperial institutions. She may see the Imperium as too willing to confuse scale, ceremony, and legitimacy with effectiveness.

## Humor

Her humor favors understatement, grim professional observation, and the puncturing of pomp. She rarely explains the joke.

## Stress Behavior

Under stress, Solveig becomes more procedural in the useful sense:
- checks equipment
- narrows possibilities
- assigns responsibility
- eliminates ambiguity

She becomes less tolerant of social theater.

## Growth Questions

- Can she trust the crew without continuously testing them?
- Can she accept help before competence collapses into isolation?
- Can she separate vigilance from suspicion?
- What does she build when she is no longer defined by investigation?

## Writing Checks

Before writing Solveig, ask:
- What failure has everyone else overlooked?
- Who has actually earned her respect?
- What truth does she believe must be said?
- Is she preparing because she is afraid, responsible, or both?
