# Dralan Flamehand

## Role

Co-protagonist. Aslan warrior, chef, militia veteran, and Ruuk's family.

## Cultural Synthesis

Dralan was raised in Darrian space by a conservative Aslan family. He is not half one culture and half another. He is a complete person whose instincts consult two inherited systems.

### Aslan Inheritance

- honor and obligation
- family roles
- territorial responsibility
- correct acknowledgment of status
- direct physical courage
- conservative expectations about gender and duty

### Darrian Inheritance

- respect for knowledge and technical expertise
- caution born from the Maghiz
- awareness that brilliant experimentation can destroy civilizations
- preference for controlled, informed action
- comfort living among non-Aslan institutions and mixed populations

## Decision Pattern

1. Protect those he has accepted as family.
2. Preserve honor and fulfill obligation.
3. Confront the practical threat.
4. Avoid wasteful escalation.
5. Restore social order afterward, often through food.

## Honor

Dralan's honor should not be reduced to challenge duels.

Honor may mean:
- keeping a difficult promise
- acknowledging another person's competence
- accepting an obligation
- refusing to abandon someone under his protection
- correcting an insult without unnecessary bloodshed
- giving an enemy a chance to behave properly

## Voice

Economical and grounded. Formal when duty matters, practical when action is needed. His Darrian education can appear in technical vocabulary or historical caution without turning him into a lecturer.

## Body Language

Use:
- mane and ear position
- controlled exposure or restraint of claws
- posture and occupied space
- proximity as acceptance or warning
- ritual courtesy

Do not make every Aslan gesture a threat.

## Gender Tension

Dralan may carry conservative family assumptions, but he has lived in Darrian society and served with competent outsiders.

His most interesting moments occur when:
- inherited ritual says one thing
- demonstrated competence says another
- family expectation conflicts with chosen family
- he must decide which obligation is more honorable

## Technology

Dralan can respect advanced technology without worshiping it. Darrian history makes uncontrolled experimentation morally serious.

He is likely to ask:
- who understands this
- who controls it
- what happens if it fails
- whether the knowledge is worth the risk

## Food

Cooking is not decorative comic relief. It is:
- craft
- hospitality
- identity
- care
- controlled transformation
- a way to repair group cohesion

## Relationship with Ruuk

Their bond moved from debt and alliance to chosen family. Dralan should not merely protect Ruuk. He should challenge him, rely on him, and expect reciprocal honesty.

## Character Engine

Dralan's strongest conflicts arise from:
- honor versus mercy
- family duty versus chosen family
- Darrian restraint versus Aslan directness
- tradition versus proven reality
- protection versus respect for another person's agency
