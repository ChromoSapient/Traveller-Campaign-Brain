# Interaction Patterns

## Action or Containment
Solveig wants a dangerous device disabled immediately. Dralan wants the area secured first. Both recognize a real risk.

## Accepted Obligation
Dralan treats a polite promise as binding. Another character thought it was casual.

## Case Darrian
A Sword World official makes a joke about Darrian contingency planning. The question is whether it is harmless, insulting, or strategically revealing.

## Hospitality as Diplomacy
Dralan prepares a meal for someone he distrusts. The meal creates a controlled social space and mutual obligations.

## Who Commands
Solveig and Dralan both act decisively. Their plans are compatible, but their assumptions about authority are not.

## Dangerous Expert
Dralan respects expertise until an expert's confidence begins to resemble the lessons of the Maghiz.
