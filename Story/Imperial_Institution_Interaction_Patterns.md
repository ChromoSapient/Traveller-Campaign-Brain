# Imperial Institution Interaction Patterns

## 1. The Favor That Is Not an Order

Ardric asks an official to reconsider a decision.

The official is free to refuse, but refusal would cost social capital.

## 2. Port versus Planet

The crew is lawful in the highport and wanted in the startown.

Moving ten meters can change jurisdiction.

## 3. Scout Credibility

A Scout contact has no formal authority but is the only person everyone trusts about a route, anomaly, or missing ship.

## 4. Naval Priority

The Navy ignores a local grievance because a strategic threat matters more.

The crew must decide whether to cooperate, escalate, or work around the institution.

## 5. TAS Access

A TAS lounge provides the right introduction, but the contact expects discretion and social competence.

## 6. Corbin's Publication Clock

Corbin can publish now with incomplete evidence or wait and risk suppression.

## 7. Competing Intelligence Services

Two services investigate the same event without sharing information.

The crew may exploit the rivalry or become trapped between incompatible orders.

## 8. The Noble Guarantee

Ardric guarantees the crew's conduct.

A later mistake becomes his reputational problem as well as theirs.

## 9. Shared Jurisdiction

A local authority and Imperial port security both claim the same prisoner, cargo, or evidence.

The legal answer matters less than who arrives first with paperwork and armed personnel.
