# Vargr Interaction Patterns

## 1. The Challenge Ruuk Does Not Recognize

A culturally fluent Vargr issues a public status challenge.

Ruuk notices aggression and audience attention but treats the matter as a practical disagreement.

Possible outcome:

- Ruuk solves the problem
- the audience awards him standing
- the challenger believes Ruuk executed a devastatingly confident counter
- Ruuk thinks everyone finally became sensible

## 2. The Accidental Concession

Ruuk disengages because the issue seems pointless.

Traditional Vargr interpret his departure as concession.

Use this when the consequence matters, not as random humiliation.

## 3. Competence Over Theater

A traditional leader makes a dramatic declaration.

Ruuk quietly organizes the actual response.

Followers begin obeying Ruuk while still formally acknowledging the other leader.

This creates unstable dual authority.

## 4. Mixed-Species Pack Blindness

Ruuk thinks of the crew as colleagues or friends.

A visiting Vargr immediately recognizes them as his pack and assumes Ruuk is its leader.

Ruuk rejects the label. The crew's behavior contradicts him.

## 5. Lurrza's Authority

A status-conscious Vargr attempts to dominate a Dustfall dispute.

Lurrza interrupts to assign emergency repair tasks.

Everyone obeys because she knows which bulkhead is failing.

Her authority is culturally local, practical, and absolute.

## 6. Teaching Charisma

A Vargr mentor explains not what Ruuk should feel, but what the audience believes it saw.

This is the correct path for Ruuk to learn.

He is not recovering lost instinct. He is studying a foreign social language built on familiar sensory inputs.
