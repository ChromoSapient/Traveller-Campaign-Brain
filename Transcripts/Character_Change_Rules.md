# Character Change Extraction Rules

## Purpose

Transcripts should not automatically rewrite personalities. They provide evidence. Changes are promoted only when the evidence is strong enough.

## Observation Levels

### Momentary Behavior
A character acts unusually because of immediate pressure. Record in the session summary, but do not change the Character Brain.

### Repeated Pattern
Similar behavior appears across multiple scenes or sessions. Add as a provisional trait with confidence 2 or 3.

### Explicit Choice
The character states a belief, makes a costly decision, or rejects a prior pattern. Update goals, values, or relationships with a source reference.

### Referee Canon
The referee explicitly establishes motive, history, or change. Record at confidence 5.

## Promotion Tests

Before changing a stable trait, ask:

1. Was the behavior chosen rather than forced?
2. Did it cost the character something?
3. Has it appeared more than once?
4. Did another character notice or react to it?
5. Does it contradict existing canon?
6. Is the apparent change better explained by stress, injury, deception, or missing context?

## Relationship Changes

Do not use numeric trust changes as game mechanics. Numbers are indexing aids.

Promote a relationship change when there is:

- voluntary vulnerability
- fulfilled costly promise
- betrayal
- rescue
- transfer of responsibility
- acceptance of another person's judgment
- explicit redefinition of the relationship

## Negative Evidence

Absence matters only cautiously. A character failing to object once does not establish approval.

## Mercedes Rule

Mercedes remains evidence-led. No stable motive, loyalty, or emotional attachment should be added without direct support.
