# Canon Methodology

## Purpose

This document defines how published Traveller lore, referee rulings, transcripts, generated prose, and speculation become usable campaign canon.

## Authority Order

1. Explicit referee ruling
2. Actual-play transcript
3. Current campaign state
4. Adopted campaign interpretation
5. Current-edition published material
6. Legacy-edition published material
7. Generated prose
8. Speculation

Higher authority does not erase lower-authority material. Conflicts are preserved with provenance until resolved.

## Three Separate Questions

Every fact must answer:

1. **What does a source say?**
2. **What interpretation does the knowledge base adopt?**
3. **What is true in this campaign?**

These answers may differ.

## Published Material

Published Traveller lore is treated as an archaeological record.

- Newer editions are not automatically superior outside rules mechanics.
- Older lore remains useful unless contradicted, superseded, or rejected.
- Multiple editions may describe the same subject differently.
- Adventures are valid lore sources for institutions, routines, locations, language, and everyday life.

## Mechanical Rules

For active play, Mongoose Traveller 2e is the default rules authority unless the referee states otherwise.

Legacy mechanics may inspire house rules but do not silently replace Mongoose 2e procedures.

## Setting Lore

Setting material may be adopted from any edition.

Adoption states:

- `adopted`: true in this campaign
- `compatible`: useful and not contradicted
- `alternate`: valid in another edition or timeline but not adopted
- `superseded`: replaced by a higher-authority source
- `rejected`: deliberately excluded
- `unresolved`: conflict requires a referee decision

## Transcript Promotion

A transcript observation becomes stable character canon only when supported by one or more of:

- explicit referee statement
- repeated behavior
- costly voluntary choice
- relationship redefinition
- direct in-character declaration
- consequences acknowledged in later play

## Generated Prose

Generated prose is provisional unless:

- it depicts already-established events without introducing new facts, or
- the referee explicitly adopts its new details.

## Negative Evidence

Silence is not canon. A source failing to mention something does not normally disprove it.

## Contradictions

Contradictions are recorded in `Campaign/Continuity_Issues.md` and linked to their source records. Never solve them by quiet editorial preference.

## Copyright

Store concise paraphrases, source references, and derived interpretations. Do not reproduce substantial copyrighted text.
